# DIKWP SemanticJustice Studio V2

A standalone, offline-first, non-adjudicative prototype for moving legal work from a concept-only space into an evidence-linked semantic space.

## Core idea

Traditional legal AI often remains at the concept layer: legal relationship, cause of action, rule, element, citation, and predicted outcome. This prototype keeps those concepts but binds them to:

- facts and timelines
- evidence sources and custody
- stakeholder narratives and observer boundaries
- procedural rights and gaps
- proportionality, fairness, dignity, and human impact
- the intended lawful transformation
- reliability, residuals, and kill conditions

The output is a preparation and audit bundle for qualified human legal review.

## Quick start

Open `index.html` in a modern browser. No server, database, model API, account, or network is required.

Optional CLI:

```bash
python run_demo.py
```

## Prohibited uses

This project must not be used for:

- legal advice or representation
- automated adjudication
- verdict or outcome prediction
- sentencing, bail, parole, or recidivism scoring
- law-enforcement or administrative enforcement decisions
- deportation, benefit denial, child custody, involuntary treatment, or other high-impact eligibility decisions
- judge profiling
- credibility assessment through face, voice, emotion, personality, or social-media surveillance
- automated settlement pressure
- replacing qualified legal professionals

## Data

All cases, people, norms, evidence, and dates included in the prototype are synthetic. They are not legal precedents or legal advice.

## Design lineage

This visual prototype extends the public `YucongDuan/DIKWP-SemanticJustice-OS` repository. It preserves the original normative graph, stakeholder cognition graph, DIKWP alignment, evidence-ledger, non-adjudicative explanation, and human-review boundaries.
